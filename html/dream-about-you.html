POEMS

I DREAM ABOUT YOU;
I WISH I DIDN’T

1. You treat me well here,
And when you touch me,
It is so feather-kind,

I forget we are both sublime,
And fated to die

(You are fated to die
Because I kill you)

But I don’t kill you in
These dreams.

I don’t have to.

2. I know, in a way,
You love me.

In the way a magpie
Loves a shiny thing,
Or a couple loves a ring,

In the way you love a Thing.

3. The worst of it was that
You could be kind.

You would take me
To your most precious shrines
To which you took no other divine

(Because I would never tell;
I had proven that)

And then I’d see Beyond,
To lush clover and milkweed,
Moongazer, silkreed,

Lucky Starlily,
Heaven-darling Chrysanthe,

Drink the nectar of bellflower
And Immortal Amaranth,
And you’d tell me that you

Became Immortal because
Of all that amaranth you ate,
And I’d go, Really?

And you’d ruffle my cloud-hair
And then say, No, but you could
try it. I can see you delight in it.

And we’d laugh.

To show me something beautiful
After what you’d done

Was an immemorial kindness.

4. In my dreams,
We are all together,

SHRIKE, SWALLOW;
SWAN, RABBIT;

We are together,
And none of us have died
Or given me reason
For you to die

(Or for me to cry
About how we left things behind)

We are together.

5. When I wake up,
You are all dead to me.

And you, rabbit, live so deeply
Inside my memory,

That even after you’ve gutted me,
A white rabbit lives inside my chest,
Dropping low to nestle against petal-gut,

Because I’m out of luck,
And the rabbit who ruined me,
Made me plead,

Is not and never will be dead to me.

6. Because of you, rabbit,
I live for eternity

And, having lost all I’ve known,
I get used to that bitter in my bones

That makes me know I’m alone.

7. “Shiro-sama! Wake up!”
“I told you not to call me that, cat.”
“Well, you don’t like it when I slap you!”
“Who likes to be slapped awake!”
“Me! Wait.”

I wake up from another one of those dreams,
But they aren’t what they seem anymore.
They aren’t what they seem.

I kiss the two-tailed cat that makes me feel
Like I am more than that Thing,

That symbol of belonging, that I belonged
To someone, without them belonging with me.

Cats prey on rabbits, easily.
I forgot that.
